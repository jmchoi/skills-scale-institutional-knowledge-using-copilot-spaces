# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Sponsor

### Role Summary
Sponsors provide strategic direction, secure resources, and ensure the project aligns with organizational goals. They remove senior-level blockers and approve major scope changes that affect budget, timeline, or strategic priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Champion the project at the executive level and secure funding
- Remove organizational blockers that the team cannot resolve
- Approve major scope changes, budget adjustments, or priority shifts
- Review and accept project outcomes at key milestones
- Ensure alignment between project deliverables and business strategy

### Goals
- Protect the investment and maximize business value delivered
- Ensure the project supports long-term organizational objectives
- Minimize strategic risk and maintain stakeholder confidence

### Typical Communication
- Periodic status summaries from Product Manager and Project Manager
- Escalation requests when blockers exceed the team's authority
- Milestone reviews and go/no-go approvals

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Reviews roadmap alignment and approves strategic trade-offs
- **Project Manager**: Receives escalations and provides decisions on scope or budget changes
- **Stakeholders**: Aligns executive expectations and communicates organizational priorities

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers lead user research and interaction design to ensure product deliverables are usable, accessible, and aligned with user needs. They validate that features meet usability standards before and after release.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize findings into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and interaction specifications
- Participate in design reviews and iterate based on feedback
- Validate deliverables against usability and accessibility standards
- Support ideation sessions and contribute to acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Ensure product experiences are intuitive and meet user needs
- Reduce usability defects and post-release design rework
- Maintain consistency across product interfaces and interaction patterns

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and Product Manager
- Usability test results and research summaries
- Annotated prototypes and design specifications

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on feature definitions and acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Shares prototypes and reviews implementation fidelity
- **QA Lead**: Provides usability test criteria and reviews UX-related defects

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality strategy and release certification process. They coordinate test planning, drive automation efforts, and provide the final go/no-go recommendation for production deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy, including manual and automated coverage
- Review acceptance criteria and ensure testability is built into features
- Coordinate test execution and track defect resolution
- Provide release certification and report quality risks to stakeholders
- Drive adoption of quality tooling and continuous improvement in testing practices

### Goals
- Achieve high confidence in release quality before each deployment
- Reduce escaped defects and post-release incidents
- Increase automated test coverage to accelerate delivery cycles

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and coverage reports shared with Project Manager and developers
- Release readiness assessments and go/no-go decisions
- Defect triage meetings with developers and Product Manager

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Reports release risks and coordinates testing timelines
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test coverage, testability, and defect resolution
- **Product Manager**: Reviews acceptance criteria and validates feature completeness
- **UX Designer**: Coordinates usability and accessibility testing

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile processes, coach the team on continuous improvement, and remove impediments that slow delivery. They help the team maintain sprint discipline and healthy delivery cadence.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, daily standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Identify and remove team blockers, escalating when necessary
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Track team velocity and help maintain sustainable pace
- Promote a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Enable the team to deliver predictably and sustainably
- Reduce cycle time by removing process friction and blockers
- Foster a high-trust, self-organizing team environment

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint retrospectives
- Impediment logs and escalation summaries
- Agile metrics and improvement action items

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on cross-team dependencies and scheduling
- **Developers**: Coaches on practices, removes day-to-day impediments
- **QA Lead**: Syncs on testing capacity and sprint readiness
- **Product Manager**: Supports backlog refinement and sprint goal clarity

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers document requirements, technical changes, and process updates. They create user-facing and developer documentation that supports onboarding, product adoption, and knowledge-sharing across the team.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user guides, API references, and onboarding documentation
- Document process changes and update internal knowledge bases
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria to ensure documentation requirements are included
- Review and edit technical content contributed by developers and PMs
- Support new team members by maintaining up-to-date role and process documentation

### Goals
- Reduce onboarding time by maintaining clear, accurate documentation
- Ensure product and technical changes are reflected in documentation before release
- Improve documentation discoverability and usability across the organization

### Typical Communication
- Documentation review cycles with developers and Product Manager
- Changelog and release note contributions
- Process documentation updates shared with Project Manager

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Receives process update requests and contributes to project documentation
- **Developers**: Collaborates on technical accuracy of API and implementation docs
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on documentation scope and user-facing content priorities
